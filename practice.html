HISTORY OF GHANA

The Rebublic of Ghana is named after the mdieval West Africa Ghana Empire. The empire became known in Europe and Arabia as the Ghana Empire after the title of its Emperor, the Ghana. The empire appears to have broken up the following 1076 conquest by Almoravid General Abu-Bakr Ibn-Umar.

Ghana was formerly known as Gold Coast. On 6th March 1957 Kwame Nkrumah declared the country's independence. On 1st July 1960, Ghana became a commonwealth republic with Mkrumah as the first President of the country. The flag of Ghana consist of the colors red,gold,green and black star.

First Tribe Who Settle In Ghana



Guans

Guans are believed to be first settlers in the modern day Ghana that migrated from the Mossi region of modern Burkina around 1000 A.D. They are scattered across all the regionsin Ghana.
